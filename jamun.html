Gulab Jamun

Gulab Jamun is a classic Indian dessert made of deep-fried milk-based dough balls soaked in aromatic sugar syrup flavored with cardamom and rose water or saffron. These soft and spongy balls melt in your mouth and are enjoyed during festivals, weddings, and celebrations. The name "Gulab" refers to rose, and "Jamun" refers to a type of berry, alluding to the dessert’s rose-flavored syrup and shape.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup khoya (mawa, evaporated milk solids)
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • ¼ teaspoon baking powder
  • 2 tablespoons milk (as needed for binding)
  • Ghee or oil for frying
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1½ cups water
  • 4-5 cardamom pods

Process

  • Combine sugar and water in a pan. Heat until the sugar dissolves, then add crushed cardamom, saffron, and rose water. Boil for 5-7 minutes, then keep it warm on low heat.
  • Crumble the khoya and mix it with flour and baking powder. Add milk gradually and knead into a soft, smooth dough. Make small, smooth balls without cracks.
  • Heat ghee or oil on low-medium heat. Fry the dough balls until golden brown, turning them to ensure even cooking.
  • Immediately place the fried gulab jamuns into the warm sugar syrup and let them soak for at least 1-2 hours.
  • Serve warm or at room temperature, optionally garnished with pistachios or almonds

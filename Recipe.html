Dosa

Dosa is a popular South Indian breakfast dish made from fermented rice and lentil batter. It's a thin, crispy crepe that can be served plain or stuffed with a variety of fillings, such as spiced mashed potatoes (aloo masala). Traditionally, dosa is served with coconut chutney and sambar, a tangy lentil soup. Its versatility and delightful texture make it a beloved choice for breakfast across India. Not only is it delicious, but dosa is also nutritious, being a good source of carbohydrates and proteins.

Ingredients
  • Rice (preferably parboiled rice or dosa rice) – 2 cups
  • Urad dal (split black gram) – ½ cup
  • Fenugreek seeds – ½ teaspoon
  • Salt – to taste
  • Water – as needed (for soaking and grinding)
  • Oil or ghee – for cooking the dosa
Making Dosa Batter
  • Rinse rice thoroughly and soak it in water for about 4–6 hours.
  • Rinse Black gram and fenugreek seeds, then soak them together in a separate bowl for 4–6 hours.
  • Drain the soaked urad dal and fenugreek seeds, and grind them in a blender or wet grinder. Add water gradually (as needed) to make a smooth, fluffy batter.
  • Next, drain the rice and grind it to a slightly coarse consistency, adding water as needed.
  • Combine the ground urad dal and rice batter in a large bowl. Stir well to form a smooth batter with a slightly thick, pouring consistency.
  • Add salt to taste.
  • Cover the batter and allow it to ferment overnight (8–12 hours) in a warm place. The batter should rise and become airy and slightly sour.
    • How to make Dosa
  • Once fermented, stir the batter gently. Heat a non-stick griddle or tawa and pour a ladleful of batter onto it. Spread it in a circular motion to make a thin dosa. Drizzle oil or ghee around the edges and cook until golden brown and crispy.

    • Note:The fermented batter can be stored in the refrigerator for 4-5 days.

Potato Curry

Indian Potato Curry, also known as Aloo Curry, is a flavorful, spiced dish made from potatoes simmered in a tangy and aromatic gravy. It is commonly served with rice, roti, dosa, or puris, and is a versatile, comfort food enjoyed across India.

Ingredients
  • 4 medium-sized potatoes (boiled and peeled)
  • 1 large onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 dried red chilies
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon urad dal (split black gram)
  • A few curry leaves
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1½ teaspoons red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnishing)
Process/Procedure
  • Boil the potatoes until they are soft, then peel and cut them into cubes or mash them lightly. Set them aside.
  • Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, urad dal, and let them splutter.
  • Add the dried red chilies and curry leaves, and sauté for a few seconds.
  • Add finely chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until the onions turn golden brown.
  • Add the ginger-garlic paste and fry for 2-3 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  • Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and coriander powder. Fry the spices for a minute to release their flavors.
  • Add the boiled and cubed or mashed potatoes. Mix well to coat the potatoes with the spice mixture.
  • Add salt to taste and cook for 5-7 minutes on low heat, stirring occasionally to avoid burning.
  • Optionally, sprinkle roasted peanut powder for a nutty flavor or add lemon juice for tanginess.
  • Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.

Chicken Curry

Chicken curry is a beloved dish across India, with each region offering its own variation. Typically, chicken is cooked in a rich, spiced gravy made from a blend of aromatic spices, onions, tomatoes, and sometimes coconut or yogurt. The curry is flavorful, with a balance of heat, tanginess, and depth, and is usually served with rice, naan, roti, or parathas.

Ingredients
  • 500g chicken (bone-in or boneless)
  • 2 onions (finely chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (finely chopped or pureed)
  • 2-3 green chilies (slit)
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 cup yogurt (optional, for marination)
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1½ teaspoons red chili powder
  • 1½ teaspoons coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1-2 bay leaves
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnishing)
  • Optional:½ cup coconut milk or cream for a richer gravy
  • In a bowl, mix the chicken pieces with yogurt, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Allow it to marinate for at least 30 minutes to an hour. This step adds flavor and tenderizes the chicken.
  • Heat oil or ghee in a large pan. Add cumin seeds, bay leaves, cardamom, cinnamon, and cloves, and sauté until aromatic
  • Add the chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until the onions become golden brown.
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 2-3 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  • Add the chopped or pureed tomatoes and cook until the mixture becomes soft and oil starts to separate from the sides.
  • Stir in turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and cumin powder. Cook the spices for a minute to release their flavors.
  • Add the marinated or plain chicken pieces. Stir well to coat the chicken in the spice mixture. Sauté for 5-7 minutes until the chicken starts to change color.
  • Add water (or coconut milk if using) to the pan, enough to create a gravy. Cover and cook the chicken for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it's tender and cooked through.
  • Adjust the consistency of the gravy by adding more water if needed.
  • Add garam masala and cook for another 2-3 minutes to enhance the flavor.
  • Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves and serve the chicken curry hot.

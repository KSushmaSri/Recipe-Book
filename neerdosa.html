Neer Dosa

Neer Dosa is a traditional, soft, and thin crepe-like dosa from the coastal regions of Karnataka, particularly popular among the Tulu-speaking community. "Neer" in Kannada means "water," and this dosa gets its name from the watery consistency of the rice batter. Unlike regular dosas, Neer Dosa doesn't require fermentation, making it quick and easy to prepare. It is light, soft, and typically served with chutney, sambar, coconut chutney, or spicy curry like chicken, fish, or egg curry. It can also be enjoyed with jaggery and coconut for a sweet touch.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup raw rice (preferably sona masoori or dosa rice)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (to make a thin, watery batter)
  • Oil (for greasing the pan)

Making Dosa Batter

  • Rinse the raw rice thoroughly and soak it in water for at least 4-5 hours or overnight.
  • Drain the soaked rice and transfer it to a blender. Add water gradually and grind it into a very smooth, watery batter. The consistency of the batter should be much thinner than regular dosa batter, almost like buttermilk. Add salt and mix well.
  • Heat a non-stick or cast-iron dosa tawa (flat griddle) on medium heat. Grease it lightly with a few drops of oil using a cloth or paper towel.
  • Once the pan is hot, pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the tawa. The batter will spread naturally due to its thin consistency. You can gently tilt the pan to spread it evenly if needed. Do not spread the batter like regular dosa; it should be thin and lacy.
  • Cover the pan with a lid and cook the dosa for about 1-2 minutes on medium heat. Unlike regular dosa, Neer Dosa is cooked only on one side. The dosa should turn white and have a soft texture. Once cooked, lift it gently with a spatula and transfer it to a plate.
  • Stir the batter before making each dosa as the rice tends to settle at the bottom. Repeat the process until all the batter is used.

Enjoy the simplicity and lightness of Neer Dosa!

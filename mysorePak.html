Mysore Pak

Mysore Pak is a traditional South Indian sweet made from gram flour, sugar, and a generous amount of ghee. Originating from the royal kitchens of Mysore, this rich, melt-in-your-mouth dessert is known for its soft, crumbly texture and indulgent flavor. It is often served during festivals like Diwali and special occasions.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • ½ cup water
  • 1 cup ghee
  • ¼ cup oil

Process

  • Sieve the gram flour to remove lumps. Heat 2 tablespoons of ghee in a pan and roast the gram flour on low heat until it turns golden and aromatic. Set aside
  • In another pan, combine sugar and water and cook until it reaches a one-string consistency (when a drop of syrup forms a single string between your fingers).
  • Gradually add the roasted gram flour into the sugar syrup, stirring continuously to prevent lumps.
  • In a separate pan, melt the ghee and oil together. Slowly pour this hot ghee-oil mixture into the flour and sugar mixture in parts, stirring constantly until it thickens and leaves the sides of the pan.
  • Pour the mixture into a greased tray, smooth the top, and allow it to cool. Once it’s slightly firm, cut into squares or diamond shapes.
  • Once fully set, serve the rich and flavorful Mysore Pak.

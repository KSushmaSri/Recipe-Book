Potato Curry

Indian Potato Curry, also known as Aloo Curry, is a flavorful, spiced dish made from potatoes simmered in a tangy and aromatic gravy. It is commonly served with rice, roti, dosa, or puris, and is a versatile, comfort food enjoyed across India.

Ingredients

  • 4 medium-sized potatoes (boiled and peeled)
  • 1 large onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 dried red chilies
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon urad dal (split black gram)
  • A few curry leaves
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1½ teaspoons red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnishing)

Process

  • Boil the potatoes until they are soft, then peel and cut them into cubes or mash them lightly. Set them aside.
  • Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, urad dal, and let them splutter.
  • Add the dried red chilies and curry leaves, and sauté for a few seconds.
  • Add finely chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until the onions turn golden brown.
  • Add the ginger-garlic paste and fry for 2-3 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  • Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and coriander powder. Fry the spices for a minute to release their flavors.
  • Add the boiled and cubed or mashed potatoes. Mix well to coat the potatoes with the spice mixture.
  • Add salt to taste and cook for 5-7 minutes on low heat, stirring occasionally to avoid burning.
  • Optionally, sprinkle roasted peanut powder for a nutty flavor or add lemon juice for tanginess.
  • Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.

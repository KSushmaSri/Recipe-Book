Fish Curry

Chicken curry is a beloved dish across India, with each region offering its own variation. Typically, chicken is cooked in a rich, spiced gravy made from a blend of aromatic spices, onions, tomatoes, and sometimes coconut or yogurt. The curry is flavorful, with a balance of heat, tanginess, and depth, and is usually served with rice, naan, roti, or parathas.

Ingredients

  • 500g fish fillets (such as kingfish, mackerel, or pomfret)
  • 2 tablespoons oil (coconut oil is ideal)
  • 1 onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (finely chopped)
  • 3-4 cloves garlic (finely chopped)
  • 1-inch piece of ginger (grated)
  • 2 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 teaspoons red chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • ½ cup tamarind pulp (or 1 teaspoon tamarind paste)
  • 1 cup coconut milk (optional for a richer flavor)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Process

  • Clean and cut the fish fillets into medium-sized pieces. Marinate with a little salt and turmeric powder. Set aside.
  • Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add curry leaves, green chilies, ginger, and garlic. Sauté until fragrant. Add chopped onions and cook until golden brown.
  • Stir in the turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and cumin powder. Sauté for a minute until the spices release their aroma.
  • Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and blend into the spice mixture.
  • Pour in the tamarind pulp and coconut milk (if using). Add salt and bring the mixture to a simmer.
  • Gently add the marinated fish pieces to the curry and cook on low heat for 8-10 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through and tender.
  • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with steamed rice or rotis.

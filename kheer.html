Kheer

Kheer is a classic Indian dessert made by slow-cooking rice, milk, and sugar until they form a creamy, luscious pudding. It is flavored with aromatic ingredients like cardamom, saffron, and garnished with nuts like almonds and pistachios. Known for its rich, silky texture, kheer is a staple during festivals, celebrations, and family gatherings. It’s not only delicious but also simple to make with ingredients readily available in most households.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup basmati rice
  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 4-5 cardamom pods (crushed)
  • A few saffron strands (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons almonds (sliced)
  • 2 tablespoons pistachios (sliced)
  • 1 tablespoon ghee (for roasting nuts)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder

Process

  • Rinse the basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for about 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  • In a heavy-bottomed pan, bring the milk to a boil on medium heat. Once it starts boiling, reduce the heat to low and let it simmer.
  • Add the soaked and drained rice to the simmering milk. Stir occasionally to prevent the milk from sticking to the bottom. Let the rice cook in the milk until it becomes soft and fully cooked (this will take around 25-30 minutes). Keep stirring regularly to ensure even cooking.
  • Once the rice is cooked and the mixture has thickened, add the sugar. Stir until the sugar dissolves completely. Add crushed cardamom pods and saffron strands (if using). Let the kheer cook for another 5-7 minutes.
  • In a small pan, heat ghee and roast the sliced almonds, pistachios, and raisins (if using) until golden. Add the roasted nuts to the kheer and stir.
  • Let the kheer simmer for a few more minutes until it reaches the desired creamy consistency. Finally, add cardamom powder for extra flavor.
  • Serve the Kheer warm or chilled, garnished with more nuts if desired.

Enjoy the simplicity and lightness of Neer Dosa!

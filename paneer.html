Palak Paneer

Palak Paneer is a popular North Indian dish made with paneer (Indian cottage cheese) cooked in a rich, smooth, and mildly spiced spinach-based gravy. The name "Palak" means spinach in Hindi, and the dish is known for its vibrant green color and creamy texture. It is both nutritious and delicious, combining the health benefits of spinach with the protein of paneer.

Ingredients

  • 250 grams fresh spinach (palak)
  • 1-2 green chilies
  • Water for blanching
  • 200 grams paneer (cubed)
  • 1 medium onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 medium tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • ½ teaspoon garam masala
  • ½ teaspoon cumin powder
  • ½ teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 tablespoon cream or yogurt (optional, for creaminess)
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (for adjusting consistency)

Procedure

  • Bring a pot of water to a boil and add the spinach leaves. Blanch them for 2-3 minutes until wilted.
  • Remove the spinach and immediately place it in ice-cold water to retain the green color.
  • Drain the spinach and blend it into a smooth puree with green chilies. Set aside.
  • Heat some oil in a pan and lightly fry the paneer cubes until golden (optional). Set them aside in warm water to keep them soft.
  • Heat 2 tablespoons of oil or ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  • Add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until the raw smell disappears.
  • Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy.
  • Add turmeric powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. Sauté for a few minutes until the spices are well cooked.
  • Add the spinach puree to the gravy and mix well. Cook for 4-5 minutes on low heat, stirring occasionally.
  • Add water as needed to adjust the consistency of the gravy.
  • Add salt to taste and stir in the cream or yogurt (optional).
  • Finally, add the paneer cubes and simmer for another 3-4 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  • Garnish with a drizzle of cream or a few drops of lemon juice for extra flavor, and serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.

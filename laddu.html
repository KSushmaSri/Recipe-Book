Motichur Laddu

Rasgulla is a beloved Bengali sweet made from chenna (Indian cottage cheese) balls soaked in a light sugar syrup. These spongy and soft white balls are known for their juicy, delicate flavor and texture. Rasgullas are especially popular during celebrationsMotichur Laddu is a popular and indulgent Indian sweet made from tiny, round, gram flour (besan) pearls or "boondis" that are fried and soaked in sugar syrup. The boondis are then shaped into soft, melt-in-the-mouth laddus. The name "Motichur" translates to "crushed pearls," referring to the fine texture of the boondi used in the sweet. These laddus are commonly prepared during festivals, religious ceremonies, and weddings, offering a rich, aromatic treat with hints of cardamom and saffron. and festivals like Durga Puja.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup gram flour (besan)
  • ¾ cup water (for batter)
  • A pinch of orange food color (optional)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • ½ cup water (for syrup)
  • 4-5 cardamom pods (crushed)
  • A few saffron strands (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (for mixing)
  • Oil or ghee for frying
  • Chopped pistachios or almonds (for garnish)

Process

  • In a bowl, mix the gram flour with water to form a smooth, lump-free batter. Add a pinch of food color if desired. The batter should be of a flowing consistency, similar to pancake batter.
  • Heat oil or ghee in a deep pan for frying. Use a perforated ladle or boondi-making ladle to pour the batter over the hot oil. Gently tap the ladle so that small droplets of batter fall into the oil and fry them into small, round boondis. Fry the boondis until just cooked (they should not turn crispy). Drain the boondis on paper towels and repeat the process with the remaining batter.
  • In a separate pan, combine sugar and ½ cup water. Boil until the syrup reaches a one-string consistency. Add crushed cardamom and saffron to enhance the flavor. Keep the syrup warm.
  • Add the fried boondis to the warm sugar syrup and mix well so that the boondis soak in the syrup evenly. Let the mixture rest for 5-10 minutes to absorb the flavors.
  • Once the mixture cools slightly and is easy to handle, add ghee and mix well. Take small portions of the boondi mixture and press them in your palms to form round laddus.
  • Garnish the Motichur Laddus with chopped pistachios or almonds and let them set for a few minutes.

Enjoy these soft, sweet Motichur laddus at any festive occasion!

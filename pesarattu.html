Pesarattu

Pesarattu is a nutritious and savory dosa made from whole green gram (moong dal) batter, originating from Andhra Pradesh and Telangana in South India. Unlike the traditional dosa, Pesarattu does not require fermentation, making it a quick and easy breakfast or snack option. It is rich in protein and fiber, making it a healthy choice. Typically served with ginger chutney or coconut chutney, Pesarattu can also be stuffed with upma, a combination known as "Upma Pesarattu."

Ingredients

  • 1 cup whole green gram (moong dal)
  • 1-inch piece ginger (roughly chopped)
  • 2-3 green chilies (adjust to taste)
  • 1 small onion (finely chopped, optional for topping)
  • 1 tablespoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon rice flour (optional, for crispiness)
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil (for greasing the pan)
  • Water (for grinding)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (chopped, optional for garnish)

Process

  • Rinse the whole green gram and soak it in water for 4-5 hours or overnight.
  • Drain the water from the soaked dal and transfer it to a blender. Add chopped ginger, green chilies, and cumin seeds. Grind the mixture into a smooth batter by adding water gradually. The batter should have a pouring consistency, similar to dosa batter. Add salt and mix well. You can also add a teaspoon of rice flour for extra crispiness.
  • Heat a non-stick or cast-iron tawa (griddle) on medium heat. Lightly grease the pan with oil.
  • Pour a ladleful of batter onto the hot tawa and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin dosa. If you prefer a thicker dosa, spread it less.
  • Drizzle a few drops of oil around the edges of the dosa. Optionally, sprinkle finely chopped onions and coriander leaves on top for extra flavor. Cook for about 2-3 minutes on medium heat until the edges turn golden and the surface looks cooked.
  • If you prefer a slightly crisp pesarattu, flip it and cook the other side for another 1-2 minutes. Otherwise, you can serve it as soon as one side is done.
  • Once cooked, remove the pesarattu from the tawa and serve it hot.

Enjoy this protein-packed, wholesome breakfast!

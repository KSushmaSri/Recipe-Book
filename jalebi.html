Jalebi

Jalebi is a popular Indian dessert, known for its crisp texture and juicy, sugary flavor. Made by deep-frying a fermented batter in circular or spiral shapes and soaking it in sugar syrup, jalebi is a classic sweet enjoyed across India, especially during festivals, weddings, and special occasions. Its bright orange color and crunchy exterior, combined with the sticky sweetness of the syrup, make it a favorite among all age groups. It is often served hot, paired with rabri (sweet condensed milk) or enjoyed on its own.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1 tablespoon gram flour (besan)
  • 1 teaspoon yogurt
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ cup water (or as needed)
  • A pinch of turmeric powder (for color) or a few drops of yellow/orange food color (optional)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • ½ cup water
  • ¼ teaspoon cardamom powder
  • ½ teaspoon lemon juice (prevents crystallization)
    • \
  • A few strands of saffron (optional, for flavor and color)
  • Ghee or oil for deep-frying

Process

  • In a bowl, mix the all-purpose flour, gram flour, yogurt, baking powder, and a pinch of turmeric or food color (optional). Add water gradually to form a smooth, thick batter.
  • The consistency should be like pancake batter—neither too runny nor too thick.
  • Cover and let the batter rest for 6-8 hours (or overnight) to ferment.
  • In a saucepan, combine sugar and water. Heat the mixture and stir until the sugar dissolves.
  • Add cardamom powder, saffron (if using), and lemon juice. Let it simmer until the syrup reaches a one-string consistency (when a drop of syrup stretched between two fingers forms a single thread).
  • Turn off the heat and keep the syrup warm.
  • Heat ghee or oil in a deep frying pan on medium heat.
  • Pour the batter into a squeeze bottle, piping bag, or a cloth with a small hole at the bottom (for easy spiraling).
  • Squeeze the batter in circular or spiral shapes directly into the hot oil.
  • Fry the jalebis until they turn golden and crisp on both sides.
  • Immediately transfer the hot fried jalebis into the warm sugar syrup. Soak them for a few seconds, ensuring they absorb the syrup, but don't let them get too soft.
  • Remove and place on a wire rack or plate.

Enjoy the crispy, syrupy Jalebi!

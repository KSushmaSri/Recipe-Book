Sambar

Sambar is a flavorful and tangy South Indian lentil-based stew, made with a variety of vegetables, spices, and tamarind. It is a staple in South Indian cuisine, often served with rice, idli, dosa, or vada. Sambar is known for its hearty, comforting flavors, combining the earthiness of lentils with the tanginess of tamarind and the aroma of roasted spices. It is rich in protein and fiber, thanks to the use of toor dal (pigeon pea lentils) and assorted vegetables like drumsticks, carrots, eggplant, and tomatoes.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup toor dal (pigeon pea lentils)
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 lemon-sized ball of tamarind
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 1 medium carrot (sliced)
  • 5-6 pieces drumsticks
  • 1 medium tomato (chopped)
  • ½ medium eggplant (cubed)
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 6-8 small shallots (optional)
  • Any other vegetables of your choice (like pumpkin or beans)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • ½ teaspoon cumin seeds
  • ¼ teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 2 dried red chilies
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 2 tablespoons sambar powder (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Procedure

  • Rinse the toor dal thoroughly and cook it in a pressure cooker with 3 cups of water, turmeric powder, and a pinch of salt. Cook for about 3-4 whistles until the dal is soft and mushy
  • Once done, mash the dal and set it aside.
  • Soak the tamarind in 1 cup of warm water for 10-15 minutes.
  • Extract the pulp by squeezing the tamarind and strain the water. Set aside.
  • In a large pot, add the chopped vegetables and 2 cups of water.
  • Add salt and a pinch of turmeric, and cook the vegetables until they are tender (but not mushy).
  • To the cooked vegetables, add the mashed dal, tamarind water, sambar powder, and red chili powder (if you want more heat).
  • Mix everything well and let the sambar simmer for about 10-12 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Adjust the consistency by adding water if necessary and check the seasoning.
  • Heat oil in a small pan. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and fenugreek seeds. Let them splutter.
  • Add dried red chilies, curry leaves, and asafoetida, and fry for a few seconds until fragrant.
  • Pour this tempering over the simmering sambar and mix well.

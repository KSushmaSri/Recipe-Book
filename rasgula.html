Rasgula

Rasgulla is a beloved Bengali sweet made from chenna (Indian cottage cheese) balls soaked in a light sugar syrup. These spongy and soft white balls are known for their juicy, delicate flavor and texture. Rasgullas are especially popular during celebrations and festivals like Durga Puja.

Ingredients

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 1-2 tablespoons lemon juice (for curdling milk)
  • 1½ cups sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • 3-4 cardamom pods (optional)

Process

  • Bring the milk to a boil, then lower the heat and add lemon juice, stirring until the milk curdles and the whey separates. Drain the curds through a muslin cloth and rinse with cold water to remove the sourness of lemon juice. Squeeze out excess water and hang the cloth for 30 minutes to get firm chenna.
  • Once the chenna is firm, transfer it to a plate and knead with your palm for 8-10 minutes until smooth and soft. Shape the chenna into small, smooth balls (around 12-15).
  • In a large pan, combine sugar and water, and bring to a boil. Add cardamom for flavor if using.
  • Add the chenna balls to the boiling sugar syrup. Cover and cook on medium heat for 15-18 minutes. The balls will double in size as they cook. Gently stir the syrup occasionally.
  • Once cooked, let the rasgullas cool in the syrup. Serve chilled or at room temperature, garnished with saffron or pistachios.

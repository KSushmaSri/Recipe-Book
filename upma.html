Upma

Upma is a popular South Indian breakfast dish made from semolina (rava or sooji), flavored with spices, vegetables, and tempering. It's a savory, wholesome dish, loved for its simplicity, quick preparation, and versatility. Upma has a soft, slightly grainy texture and is often accompanied by chutney or sambar. It is light yet filling, making it a preferred breakfast or snack option.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup semolina (rava or sooji)
  • 1 tablespoon ghee or oil
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1 teaspoon chana dal (split Bengal gram)
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 1-2 dried red chilies
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 1-2 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 medium onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 ½ cups water
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice (optional)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Procedure

  • Heat a tablespoon of ghee or oil in a pan. Add the semolina and roast it on low heat, stirring continuously until it turns light golden and emits a nutty aroma (about 4-5 minutes).
  • Set the roasted semolina aside in a bowl.
  • In the same pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil or ghee. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  • Add urad dal, chana dal, and a pinch of asafoetida. Fry until the dals turn golden brown.
  • Add dried red chilies, curry leaves, and slit green chilies. Sauté for a few seconds.
  • Add chopped onions and grated ginger, and sauté until the onions turn soft and translucent.
  • Add 2 ½ cups of water to the pan. Add salt and bring the water to a rolling boil.
  • Once the water boils, lower the heat and gradually add the roasted semolina while stirring continuously to prevent lumps from forming.
  • Stir the mixture until the semolina absorbs all the water and the upma thickens.
  • Cover the pan and let the upma cook on low heat for 2-3 minutes.

Enjoy your soft, flavorful Upma!

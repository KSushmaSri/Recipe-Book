Pulihora

Pulihora, also known as Tamarind Rice, is a traditional South Indian dish that's tangy, spicy, and flavorful. It is a staple during festivals, temple offerings (prasadam), and special occasions in homes across Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Karnataka, and Tamil Nadu. The name "Pulihora" comes from two words: "Puli," meaning tamarind, and "Hora," meaning rice, highlighting its main ingredients.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice (cooked and cooled)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil (or any oil)
  • 1 small lemon-sized ball of tamarind
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 green chilies (slit)
  • 2 dried red chilies
  • 1 teaspoon jaggery (optional, to balance the tanginess)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon chana dal (split Bengal gram)
  • 1 teaspoon urad dal (split black gram)
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 1 sprig of curry leaves
  • 3-4 green chilies (slit)
  • 2 dried red chilies
  • 2 tablespoons peanuts (roasted)

Making

  • Soak tamarind in warm water for about 15-20 minutes.
  • Extract the thick tamarind pulp and set it aside.
  • Heat a tablespoon of oil in a pan and add the tamarind pulp, turmeric, salt, slit green chilies, and red chilies.
  • Cook on low heat until the raw smell of tamarind goes away and the paste thickens (around 8-10 minutes).
  • Add jaggery at this stage if you want a slightly sweet flavor.
  • Cook the rice and allow it to cool completely. Drizzle some sesame oil to prevent it from sticking.
  • In a pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil and add mustard seeds.
  • Once the mustard seeds splutter, add cumin seeds, chana dal, urad dal, peanuts, and saut√© until golden brown.
  • Add asafoetida, curry leaves, slit green chilies, and dried red chilies. Fry for a minute.
  • Add the prepared tamarind paste to the tempering and mix well. Let it cook for a couple of minutes.
  • Remove from heat and mix this paste with the cooled rice, making sure everything is evenly coated.
  • Allow the pulihora to rest for 30 minutes so that the flavors meld together.

Enjoy your delicious, tangy Pulihora!
